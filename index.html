Men's mental health month

More info

June is mens mental health awareness month. 600,000 men die by suicide each year, that is 1,644 men per day and 68 men per hour. It is important to spread awareness about mental health and the resources available.

Reach out.

If you or someone you know is struggling with mental health, reach out for help. "I would rather have a friend cry on my shoulder, then have to cry at their funeral next week." - Paddy Pimblett UFC Fighter

Build Healthy Habits

Getting enough sleep, eating a balanced diet, and exercising regularly can help improve your mental health. You should take regular walks and engage in activities that you enjoy.

In an emergency?

The national suicide prevention lifeline is available 24/7 at 988.

In need of help or support?

Contact a mental health professional or visit your local hospital for assistance. Just like the body requires care, your mental health and mind need care aswell. Seeking help is a sign of strength not weakness. Talking to a professional can help provide support, guidence, and helpful coping strategies.

How can you help?

  • Start a conversation with someone you care about and ask how they're doing.

    • Be a good listener and offer support without judgment.

  • Encourage them to seek help if they're struggling.

    • Help them find professional support if needed.

  • Educate yourself about mental health and the resources available.

    • Use websites like the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline or Mental Health America for more information.

  • Donate to organizations that support mental health research and advocacy.

    • Donations can make a big difference in supporting mental health initiatives.
